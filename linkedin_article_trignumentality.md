## What is Trignumentality?

**Trignumentality** is a new philosophical-technical doctrine — the first unified framework that synthesizes Kantian transcendental philosophy with the technical implementation of AI validation systems.

In simple terms: it answers the question *"How do we know an AI output can be trusted?"* — not with a confidence score, but with a principled framework that runs in code.

It rests on five principles:

**1. The Raw Material Principle**
AI outputs are not knowledge. They are raw material — like clay before the potter. Every output must be processed before it becomes a basis for action.

**2. The Technical A Priori Principle**
Validation must occur *before* execution, not after. Post-hoc auditing is not enough. The conditions for valid action must be verified *before* the action happens.

**3. The Human Sovereignty Principle**
The human is the final judge. No matter how sophisticated the validation system, the ultimate decision belongs to the human. Machines process. Humans decide.

**4. The Epistemic Integration Principle**
Genuine knowledge in the AI age emerges from the integration of three poles:
```
Human Reason + AI Outputs + Sensible World = Genuine Knowledge
```

**5. The Ultimate Reference Principle**
The sensible world is the final boundary. All AI outputs must be ultimately traceable to empirical reality. No purely symbolic output can claim truth without grounding.
